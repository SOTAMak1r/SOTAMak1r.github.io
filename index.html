Controllable and Editable 3D Scene Generation with Multi-Layer Image Decomposition

CONE is a two-stage text-to-3D scene generation model.

Abstract

3D generative AI presents an innovative and effective approach to crafting a large-scale 3D content base. Existing 3D generation works primarily concentrate on devising methods for producing high-fidelity 3D assets. Yet, in practice, practitioners seek not only quality but also the capacity to generate content with intricate descriptions and easy editing, which current methods fail to accommodate.

In this paper, we propose a novel pipeline for scene-level 3D generation, which is, to the best of our knowledge, the first controllable and editable method without pre-defined 3D structures. Specifically, given a pre-defined prompt as a control condition, such as a descriptive sentence or a design image, the proposed method can synthesize a 3D scene that meets the given requirements such as artistic style and scene arrangement. Upon completion of the generation, creators can effortlessly modify the generated 3D scene, significantly streamlining the process of attaining the desired 3D scenes. The key to our proposed pipeline lies in formulating the 3D scene generation problem as a two-stage generation problem and leveraging an off-the-shelf mask segmentation model (SAM) to decompose the intermediate 2D image into a layered image representation. Extensive experiments demonstrate that the proposed pipeline is highly adept at generating high-quality, editable 3D scenes in accordance with user prompts. Code will be released upon acceptance.
